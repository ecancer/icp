## About the forum

The Global Cancer Policy Forum is the Institute of Cancer Policy's flagship annual gathering — a full-day conference bringing together researchers, clinicians, health ministers, and international policy leaders to examine the most pressing challenges in cancer policy worldwide.

The 2026 forum will focus on the theme **Closing the Gap: from equity commitments to structural change**.

## Programme highlights

**Morning plenary: The state of global cancer equity**
Keynote address by the WHO Director-General for Non-Communicable Diseases, followed by a panel of health ministers from Nigeria, India, and Brazil.

**Workshop stream A: Financing cancer care in LMICs**
A practical workshop on domestic resource mobilisation, development finance, and the role of philanthropic capital in building sustainable cancer systems.

**Workshop stream B: Making drug access work**
Case studies from Kenya, Colombia, and Indonesia on compulsory licensing, local manufacturing, and managed entry agreements.

**Afternoon plenary: The research–policy interface**
What does it take to turn evidence into action? A candid conversation with senior policymakers about what makes research useful — and what doesn't.

**Closing session: The Forum Declaration**
Participants will discuss and adopt the 2026 Forum Declaration on Cancer Equity, to be presented to the WHO World Health Assembly in May.
