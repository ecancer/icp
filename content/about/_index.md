## Our mission

The Institute of Cancer Policy at King's College London is a world-leading centre for research, policy analysis, and advocacy on the global cancer burden. We work at the intersection of science, health systems, and political will — translating rigorous evidence into actionable policy recommendations for governments, international bodies, and health systems around the world.

## Why we exist

Every year, 10 million people die from cancer. The majority of those deaths are preventable or treatable — yet they occur because the right medicines, diagnostics, and care are not available to the people who need them. This is not primarily a problem of science. It is a problem of policy.

The Institute was founded in 2008 on the conviction that the global cancer community needed a dedicated institution focused not just on generating evidence, but on ensuring that evidence changes how decisions are made. We have been doing that work for nearly two decades.

## Our home

We are based at King's College London, one of the world's leading research universities and a founding partner of King's Health Partners — an academic health sciences centre bringing together world-class clinical care, research, and education in the heart of London.

Our location at the Denmark Hill campus places us within one of the highest-density concentrations of cancer clinical expertise in Europe, including King's College Hospital, Guy's Cancer Centre, and the Francis Crick Institute nearby.

## Funding and independence

The Institute is funded through a combination of King's College London core funding, research grants from major funders including the Wellcome Trust, Cancer Research UK, the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, and the National Institute for Health and Care Research (NIHR), and unrestricted philanthropic gifts.

We do not accept funding from the pharmaceutical industry for our policy work. Our research grant income from industry is subject to a strict independence policy, which is published in full on our website.
